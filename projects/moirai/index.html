Moirai is the systems side of the embodied AI goal: train reinforcement-learning policies in flexible tooling, then deploy them on constrained hardware without dragging a full ML framework into the robot.

The design target is a specialized runtime for fixed-shape control MLPs: no heap allocation, compact ternary or 2-bit weights, cache-resident policy data, and explicit vectorized kernels. It is intentionally narrow because robots need predictable latency more than a generalized inference server.

The source stays private while the training and quantization methodology stabilizes, but the direction is clear: make policy execution small, inspectable, deterministic, and close to the metal.
